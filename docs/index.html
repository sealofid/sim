How each trait affects energy efficiency

Energy is in food units (1 pellet = 1). Each tick a creature spends metabolism + movement + sensing + storage upkeep and eats to refill — run out and it dies. Every trait buys an advantage but raises one of those costs, so selection chases the best net balance.

TraitWhat it buysEnergy costMost efficient when…
speed Covers more ground per tick — reaches food and prey sooner and wins contested food. moving ∝ speed² food is contested; wasteful once you're already well-fed.
sense Detects food and prey within a larger radius — heads straight to them instead of wandering. sensing ∝ sense² food is sparse; pure waste when food is dense (why it stays flat here).
size Can eat creatures ≥1.2× smaller and steal their stored calories; harder to be eaten; bigger storage cap. metabolism ∝ size² + drag ∝ size there's enough smaller prey to hunt to offset the higher upkeep.
reserve Raises the max energy it can bank (cap = base + per_size·size·reserve) to stockpile surplus food. upkeep ∝ size·reserve / tick famine looms; dead weight in plenty, lifesaver in a drought.
